MOBILE PHILOSOPHY


Mobile: the culmination of hundreds of years of innovation into the most profound technological achievement of our lifetime.

Mobile is disrupting mass-media, politics, education, business and how people consume information. Everybody in society is affected by mobile devices.

According to comScore in 2014 approximately 182 million Americans owned a smartphone, representing 75% of the population.


This technology is not just limited to smartphones; mobile incorporates wearable devices, automobiles, toys, and even portable smart appliances. Increasingly, platforms that would not, at first, appear to be mobile, such as home video game systems, cable boxes, and Blu-Ray players are using mobile-derived operating systems. The Sony Playstation store, Android TV, Samsung Smart TV, and Apple TV are examples of mobile operating systems applied to the home market.


"mobile phones now account for 62% of all time spent online... 44% of time is spent on smartphone apps" - Wall Street Journal

From a one-man startup to a billion dollar operation, a poor mobile strategy is increasingly pointing towards disconnect with customers.

MOBILE DESIGN


A positive user experience can make or break an application. The user should know within seconds of opening the app how to navigate around. Each screen should clearly define the purpose of the application and menu options should be ke

Movements should be intuitive and flawless. simple experience that takes advantage of the mobile platform and the strength of the individual machine. I prefer native development above other platforms because of the flexibility and extra functionality it allows the designer and the challenge of stringing together large, complex application logic. Some applications require a complex physics engine (like a game) or are built upon a hybrid model which displays elements of webpages. Native development allows complete use of the device features as well as location services, a localized database, and rock-solid security. The options for end-users to spend money on an app are also nearly limitless.
